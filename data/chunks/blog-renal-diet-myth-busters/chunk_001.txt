Navigating through all the different information can be confusing and overwhelming, but we are here to help! We are sharing some widespread myths and facts related to renal diet, which will assist you in formulating an effective, balanced diet strategy. Myth: There is only one renal diet that everyone on dialysis needs to follow. Fact: Everyone is unique, and that includes their dietary needs, which depend on some of the following: Stage of kidney disease. Lab results. Medications. Comorbidities like diabetes or high blood pressure. Lifestyle activities like exercising. Working with your healthcare team and renal dietitian is the best way to create the right diet plan for you. Myth: Whole grains, nuts, seeds, and beans have too much phosphorus. Fact: Whole grains, nuts, seeds, and beans contain phosphorus, but your body only absorbs 50% of phosphorus from non-processed, plant-based foods! You absorb 80-100% of phosphorus from protein-rich foods like meat, fish, and dairy or processed foods with phosphorus additives. Avoid phosphorus additives like Sodium hexametaphosphate, Dicalcium phosphate, Trisodium phosphate, Sodium tripolyphosphate, and Phosphoric acid. Myth: People with kidney disease need a low-potassium diet. Fact: Individuals with kidney disease are often thought to need a low-potassium diet.
