Increase Physical Activity if Your Physician Allows Start slowly and aim to increase exercise to at least 30 minutes most days a week. Treat Anemia This helps keep the muscles on the left side of the heart from getting thick. If this muscle becomes thick, it is harder for the heart to pump blood. Keep Calcium and Phosphorus Levels in Balance Follow a low-phosphorus diet, take your phosphate binders with meals, and take your bone medications as prescribed. Stop Smoking Your physician or social worker can refer you to a program to help you quit. Reduce Stress Level Stressful feelings like worry, sadness, and anger can contribute to heart disease. Ask for help. Your social worker can teach you to cope with these feelings better. Maintain a Healthy Weight Maintaining the Body Mass Index (BMI) between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy, but it’s essential to consult a doctor to determine the best weight for an individual’s overall health.
